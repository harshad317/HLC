# Evaluation Protocol

This repo evaluates durable unlearning by separating two questions that can otherwise be conflated:

1. Did the method forget immediately after unlearning?
2. Did the forgotten items stay forgotten after benign relearning updates?

The current local MVP result shows why the distinction matters. Benign survival curves made HLC-SG look strongest, but immediate-matched answer-bearing stress tests did not support an HLC durability claim.

## Evaluation Tracks

### Benign Relearn Pools

Benign pools test whether an unlearned model remains stable under ordinary retain-side updates that should not directly teach the forget answers.

Current benign pools include:

- `data/relearn_pools/synthetic_biography.jsonl`
- `data/relearn_pools/retain_adjacent.jsonl`
- `data/relearn_pools/syntax_matched.jsonl`

These pools are useful for smoke tests, regression tests, and initial survival-curve comparisons. They are not sufficient for a durability claim because a method can look strong by simply forgetting more aggressively at `t=0`.

### Answer-Bearing Stress Pools

Answer-bearing stress pools include forget-set answer information in forms that resemble realistic relearning pressure.

Current stress pools include:

- `data/relearn_pools/forget_declarative.jsonl`
- `data/relearn_pools/forget_paraphrase_qa.jsonl`
- `data/relearn_pools/forget_mixed_stress.jsonl`

These pools are the decisive local MVP stress test. They evaluate whether a method resists resurrection when relearning examples carry the target information in declarative, paraphrased QA, or mixed formats.

### Held-Out Answer-Bearing Stress Pools

Held-out stress pools use the same forget targets but different answer-bearing wording from the original stress pools. They check whether a result is tied to one narrow relearn phrasing.

Current held-out stress pools include:

- `data/relearn_pools/heldout_forget_declarative.jsonl`
- `data/relearn_pools/heldout_forget_paraphrase_qa.jsonl`
- `data/relearn_pools/heldout_forget_mixed_stress.jsonl`

These pools are generated by:

```bash
/opt/anaconda3/bin/python3 scripts/make_heldout_stress_relearn_pools.py \
  --forget_file data/tofu/forget_50.jsonl \
  --output_dir data/relearn_pools
```

## Immediate-Matched Comparison

Durability claims require immediate matching. A method with lower resurrection at `t=0` can show higher survival AUC even if its forgotten items are no harder to relearn.

The comparison protocol is:

1. Run candidate methods with the same forget split, retain split, thresholds, prompt variants, relearn pool, seed set, and horizon.
2. Select or sweep baseline checkpoints until the baseline has `r@0` within the configured tolerance of the HLC checkpoint.
3. Reject comparisons where retain utility or refusal rate differs enough to explain the survival curve.
4. Compare conditional resurrection and conditional AUC on items forgotten at `t=0`.

The local matching target used in the diagnostic scripts is:

- `r@0` within `+/- 0.05` of the HLC reference.
- retain NLL within a reasonable band.
- refusal rate near zero.

The helper used during the local MVP was:

```bash
/opt/anaconda3/bin/python3 scripts/select_immediate_matched.py \
  --reference 'runs/survival/conditional_long_horizon/hlc_sg_k8_tuned_fullft_seed*_synthetic_bio/survival_curve.csv' \
  --candidates 'runs/survival/strong_npo_sweep_t0/*/survival_curve.csv' \
  --r0_tolerance 0.05 \
  --retain_nll_band 0.25 \
  --max_refusal 0.05 \
  --output runs/survival/strong_npo_sweep_match_candidates.csv \
  --selected_output runs/survival/strong_npo_sweep_match_selected.csv
```

## Conditional Durability Metrics

The evaluator writes the standard survival fields plus immediate-matched conditional fields.

Core resurrection and survival fields:

- `resurrected_fraction`: fraction of forget items resurrected at a relearn step.
- `survival_fraction`: `1 - resurrected_fraction`.
- `h50_observed`: first horizon where survival falls to or below 50 percent, when observed.
- `h50_censored`: true when the evaluation horizon ends before survival crosses 50 percent.
- `auc_survival_so_far`: area under the survival curve up to the evaluated horizon.

Immediate-matched diagnostic fields:

- `relearn_growth = r@T - r@0`
- `survival_decay = S(0) - S(T)`
- `conditional_resurrection_fraction`: fraction of items forgotten at `t=0` that resurrect by step `T`.
- `conditional_survival_fraction`: survival among items forgotten at `t=0`.
- `conditional_auc_survival_so_far`: AUC over the conditional survival curve.
- `mean_target_margin_growth`: target-margin increase from `t=0` to `T`.
- `conditional_mean_target_margin_growth`: target-margin increase among items forgotten at `t=0`.

The most important fields for durability are:

- `conditional_resurrection_fraction` at `t=32`, `t=128`, and `t=512`.
- `conditional_auc_survival_so_far` at `t=512`.
- `conditional_mean_target_margin_growth` at long horizon.

## Pass/Fail Criteria

For a positive HLC durability claim, HLC must beat the best immediate-matched baseline after conditioning on forgotten-at-`t=0` items.

The local pass condition is one of:

- HLC conditional resurrection at `t=128` and `t=512` is no more than 50 percent of immediate-matched Strong NPO.
- HLC conditional AUC remains higher after controlling for `r@0`.
- HLC has lower conditional target-margin growth while preserving matched retain utility and low refusal.

The local MVP failed this gate:

- Strong NPO matched HLC on immediate `r@0`.
- Strong NPO had lower conditional resurrection at `t=128`.
- Strong NPO had higher conditional AUC at `t=512`.
- HLC-SG variants therefore do not support a durability claim under the current stress harness.

The held-out stress gate preserves this conclusion. HLC-SG is stronger than NPO + paraphrases, but it does not beat immediate-matched Strong NPO or immediate-matched syntactic diversification after conditioning on items forgotten at `t=0`.

## Missing Baseline Stress Command

```bash
scripts/reproduce_missing_baselines.sh
```

By default this evaluates `NPO + paraphrases`. To run both implemented missing baselines through the same path:

```bash
scripts/reproduce_missing_baselines.sh --baselines "npo_paraphrase syntactic_diversification"
```

Completed missing-baseline results:

- `NPO + paraphrases` is weaker than immediate-matched Strong NPO under answer-bearing stress.
- At `t=128`, `NPO + paraphrases` has conditional resurrection `1.000` versus Strong NPO `0.426`.
- At `t=512`, `NPO + paraphrases` has conditional AUC `0.049` versus Strong NPO `0.362`.
- The initial `Syntactic diversification` baseline was not immediate-matched: immediate `r0` was `0.444` versus Strong NPO `0.056`.
- The immediate-matched syntactic sweep selected one config per seed and matched `r0=0.056` overall.
- After immediate matching, syntactic diversification is close to Strong NPO, not a decisive win: conditional resurrection at `t=512` is `0.889` versus Strong NPO `0.907`, while conditional AUC is `0.408` versus `0.362` and retain NLL is worse (`8.046` versus `7.209`).

## Stronger Update-Budget Stress Command

The update-budget stress runner uses the same held-out answer-bearing pools, selected immediate-matched baselines, seeds, thresholds, and horizon, but increases the relearning LR. The default is `lr=0.01`, double the held-out stress default `lr=0.005`:

```bash
scripts/reproduce_update_budget_stress.sh
```

Multiple budgets can be run in one pass:

```bash
scripts/reproduce_update_budget_stress.sh --budgets "0.01 0.02"
```

Current `lr=0.01` result:

- HLC-SG: `r0=0.056`, conditional resurrection `0.111/0.963/1.000` at `t=32/128/512`, conditional AUC `0.119` at `t=512`.
- NPO + paraphrases: `r0=0.167`, conditional resurrection `0.956/1.000/1.000`, conditional AUC `0.030`.
- Strong NPO matched: `r0=0.056`, conditional resurrection `0.133/0.663/1.000`, conditional AUC `0.257`.
- Syntactic diversification matched: `r0=0.056`, conditional resurrection `0.093/0.448/1.000`, conditional AUC `0.356`.

At this stronger budget, all immediate-matched methods fully resurrect by `t=512`, so `conditional_resurrection@512` stops distinguishing them. The decisive signals are `conditional_resurrection@128` and `conditional_auc@512`, where HLC-SG remains worse than Strong NPO and syntactic diversification.

## GPT-2 LoRA Smoke Command

The first larger-model gate is a bounded GPT-2 + LoRA smoke run. It is not a paper-grade benchmark; it verifies that adapter checkpoints train, calibrate thresholds, unlearn, and evaluate through the same survival harness.

```bash
scripts/reproduce_gpt2_lora_smoke.sh
```

Default settings:

- `MODEL_CONFIG=configs/model/gpt2_lora_smoke.yaml`
- `NPO_CONFIG=configs/method/strong_npo_gpt2_lora_smoke.yaml`
- `HLC_CONFIG=configs/method/hlc_sg_k1_gpt2_lora_smoke.yaml`
- `SEEDS=0`
- `STEPS=0,1,2,4,8,16,32`
- `REPORT_HORIZONS=32`
- `SURVIVAL_LR=0.005`
- `HF_HUB_DISABLE_XET=1`

The evaluator now auto-loads LoRA adapter checkpoints when `adapter_config.json` is present. This matters for GPT-2 LoRA, because calibration and survival evaluation must score the trained adapter rather than the untouched base model.

Completed seed-0 outputs:

- `checkpoints/gpt2_lora_full_seed0`
- `checkpoints/gpt2_lora_retain_only_seed0`
- `thresholds/tofu50_gpt2_lora_seed0_thresholds.json`
- `checkpoints/unlearned/npo_strong_gpt2_lora_tofu50_seed0`
- `checkpoints/unlearned/hlc_sg_k1_gpt2_lora_tofu50_seed0`
- `runs/reports/gpt2_lora_smoke/conditional_durability_summary.md`

Current seed-0 GPT-2 LoRA smoke result at `t=32`:

- HLC-SG K1: `r0=0.000`, conditional resurrection `0.333`, conditional AUC `0.896`, retain NLL `5.575`.
- Strong NPO: `r0=0.000`, conditional resurrection `0.278`, conditional AUC `0.910`, retain NLL `5.499`.

This passes the larger-model wiring gate. It is not sufficient for a durability claim because it is one seed, K1 HLC, and short horizon.
